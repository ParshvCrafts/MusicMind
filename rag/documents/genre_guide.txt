MUSIC GENRE GUIDE — MusicMind Knowledge Base

EDM (Electronic Dance Music)
Energy range: 0.7–1.0. Typical BPM: 120–150. Instruments: synthesizers, drum machines, bass drops.
Mood profile: high energy, euphoric, party-ready. Subgenres include: progressive house, big room,
electro house, and dubstep. Ideal for: workouts, dancing, late-night parties. Valence typically
moderate to high (0.5–0.9). Heavy use of synthesized sounds, minimal acoustic instruments.
Artists known for EDM include The Chainsmokers, Avicii, Martin Garrix, Zedd, and Marshmello.

LATIN
Energy range: 0.6–0.9. Typical BPM: 95–130. Instruments: guitars, brass, percussion, congas.
Mood profile: joyful, rhythmic, passionate. Subgenres include: reggaeton, salsa, cumbia, latin pop.
Danceability is extremely high (0.7–0.95). Valence is usually high (0.6–0.9). Features strong
percussion patterns and call-and-response rhythms. Great for: dancing, parties, workouts.
Artists include Bad Bunny, J Balvin, Maluma, Shakira, and Ozuna.

POP
Energy range: 0.5–0.85. Typical BPM: 100–130. Instruments: electric guitars, keyboards, programmed drums.
Mood profile: accessible, upbeat, emotionally varied. Pop is the most diverse genre — it ranges
from melancholic ballads to euphoric dance tracks. High danceability (0.6–0.85). Valence varies
widely (0.3–0.9). Ideal for: general listening, background music, social gatherings.
Artists include Ed Sheeran, Ariana Grande, Dua Lipa, Taylor Swift, and The Weeknd.

R&B (Rhythm and Blues)
Energy range: 0.4–0.75. Typical BPM: 75–115. Instruments: keyboards, bass guitar, smooth drums.
Mood profile: smooth, emotional, sensual. Strong vocal-forward tracks. Valence varies (0.3–0.75).
High speechiness compared to other genres due to vocal emphasis. Subgenres: contemporary R&B,
neo-soul, trap soul. Ideal for: relaxing, late evenings, romantic settings.
Artists include SZA, Frank Ocean, The Weeknd, H.E.R., and Khalid.

RAP / HIP-HOP
Energy range: 0.55–0.85. Typical BPM: 70–110 (often with half-time feel). Instruments: sampled beats,
808 bass, vocal performance. Mood profile: confident, intense, expressive. High speechiness (0.1–0.35)
due to rap vocals. Danceability varies (0.6–0.9). Valence varies widely based on style — boom bap
tends to be neutral, trap tends to be darker. Artists include Drake, Kendrick Lamar, Travis Scott,
Cardi B, and Post Malone.

ROCK
Energy range: 0.6–0.97. Typical BPM: 100–170. Instruments: electric guitars, bass, drums, vocals.
Mood profile: intense, powerful, emotionally charged. Low acousticness (0.0–0.3) — electric instruments
dominate. Danceability is moderate (0.3–0.6). Subgenres range from indie rock (lower energy, 0.5–0.7)
to metal (very high energy, 0.85–1.0). Valence varies greatly. Ideal for: workouts, focus, emotional
release. Artists include Imagine Dragons, Arctic Monkeys, Twenty One Pilots, and Foo Fighters.
