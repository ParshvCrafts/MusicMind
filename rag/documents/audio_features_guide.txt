SPOTIFY AUDIO FEATURES GUIDE — MusicMind Knowledge Base

This guide explains the 12 audio features used by Spotify's audio analysis engine.
All numerical features are normalized to the range 0.0 to 1.0 unless otherwise noted.

DANCEABILITY (0.0–1.0)
Describes how suitable a track is for dancing based on tempo, rhythm stability, beat strength,
and overall regularity. 0.0 = least danceable, 1.0 = most danceable. Tracks above 0.8 are
excellent for dancing. EDM and latin music typically score 0.7–0.95. Acoustic ballads score 0.2–0.4.

ENERGY (0.0–1.0)
Perceptual measure of intensity and activity. High-energy tracks feel fast, loud, and noisy.
Contributing factors: dynamic range, perceived loudness, timbre, onset rate. Death metal scores
near 1.0. Classical nocturnes score near 0.1. This is the single most important feature for
matching activity-based queries (workout, sleep, coding).

VALENCE (0.0–1.0)
Musical positiveness. High valence = sounds positive (happy, cheerful, euphoric).
Low valence = sounds negative (sad, depressed, angry). This is NOT volume or energy — a loud
rock song can have low valence (angry) or high valence (triumphant). Key for matching emotional
state of the user.

ACOUSTICNESS (0.0–1.0)
Confidence measure of whether the track is acoustic. 1.0 = high confidence it is acoustic.
Electric guitars, synthesizers, and drum machines push this toward 0.0. Acoustic guitars,
piano, and orchestral instruments push toward 1.0. Important for matching "acoustic preference."

INSTRUMENTALNESS (0.0–1.0)
Predicts whether a track contains no vocals. "Ooh" and "aah" sounds are treated as instrumental.
Rap and spoken word tracks score near 0.0. Purely instrumental classical or electronic scores near 1.0.
Values above 0.5 indicate likely instrumental. Highly relevant for focus/coding contexts where
lyrics compete with reading.

SPEECHINESS (0.0–1.0)
Detects presence of spoken words. Above 0.66 = mostly spoken words (podcast, audiobook, spoken word).
Between 0.33 and 0.66 = may contain both music and speech (rap). Below 0.33 = music with little speech.
For concentration tasks, prefer tracks with speechiness < 0.1.

TEMPO (BPM)
Overall estimated tempo in beats per minute. NOT normalized — raw BPM float.
Typical ranges: ambient 40–70 BPM, chill 70–90 BPM, mid-tempo 90–120 BPM,
upbeat 120–140 BPM, intense 140–180 BPM.

LIVENESS (0.0–1.0)
Detects presence of an audience in the recording. Above 0.8 = strong likelihood of live recording.
Most studio tracks score below 0.3. Live recordings have different acoustic signatures —
more room ambience, crowd noise, and dynamic variation.

KEY (0–11)
Estimated overall key using standard Pitch Class notation: 0=C, 1=C#/Db, 2=D, 3=D#/Eb,
4=E, 5=F, 6=F#/Gb, 7=G, 8=G#/Ab, 9=A, 10=A#/Bb, 11=B. -1 = no key detected.

MODE (0 or 1)
Modality of the track. 1 = major scale (typically sounds brighter, happier).
0 = minor scale (typically sounds darker, more melancholic). Major keys often correlate
with higher valence, minor keys with lower valence, though this is not universal.

LOUDNESS (dB)
Overall loudness averaged across the entire track. NOT normalized — raw dB float.
Typical range: -60 to 0 dB. Quiet acoustic music: -25 to -15 dB. Loud rock/EDM: -8 to -3 dB.

DURATION_MS
Duration of the track in milliseconds. Typical pop song: 180,000–240,000 ms (3–4 minutes).
