ACTIVITY TO MUSIC MAPPING — MusicMind Knowledge Base

CODING / PROGRAMMING
Target energy: 0.35–0.55. Target valence: 0.4–0.65.
Ideal BPM: 80–110. Instrumentalness preference: HIGH (0.7–1.0).
Speechiness preference: LOW (avoid lyrics that compete with reading code).
Best genres: EDM (melodic house), lo-fi hip-hop, ambient electronic, post-rock.
Best moods: focused, chill, ambient. Avoid: intense, party (too distracting).
Rationale: Coding requires sustained attention. Lyrics activate language centers that
compete with reading. Steady mid-energy beats provide rhythmic support without disruption.
The "flow state" is best maintained by tracks that don't demand conscious attention.

WORKING OUT / GYM
Target energy: 0.8–1.0. Target valence: 0.5–0.9.
Ideal BPM: 120–160. Danceability: moderate to high.
Best genres: EDM, rock, rap, latin. Best moods: happy, intense, party.
Avoid: ambient, chill, sad (drains motivation).
Rationale: High energy and high BPM synchronize with physical exertion. The drive of
intense or happy music raises adrenaline and reduces perceived exertion during workouts.
Many athletes prefer tracks at 140–160 BPM for high-intensity intervals.

STUDYING / READING
Target energy: 0.25–0.45. Target valence: 0.35–0.65.
Ideal BPM: 60–100. Instrumentalness preference: HIGH.
Best genres: Classical, acoustic, lo-fi. Best moods: chill, focused, ambient.
Avoid: rap (high speechiness), rock (high energy disrupts reading).
Rationale: Similar to coding but even lower energy. Reading comprehension drops when
music has competing lyrics. Steady rhythms without surprise tempo changes work best.

SLEEP / WIND DOWN
Target energy: 0.0–0.25. Target valence: 0.35–0.65 (neutral to slightly positive).
Ideal BPM: below 70. Acousticness: HIGH.
Best genres: Ambient, acoustic, classical. Best moods: ambient, chill, sad (gentle).
Avoid: anything with energy > 0.5 or BPM > 90.
Rationale: Music for sleep should slow the nervous system. BPM below the resting heart
rate (~60–70 BPM) has been shown to reduce heart rate and promote relaxation. Silence-heavy
tracks with long decay work especially well.

COMMUTING / DRIVING
Target energy: 0.55–0.8. Target valence: 0.5–0.8.
Ideal BPM: 100–130. No strong instrumentalness preference.
Best genres: Pop, rock, R&B, latin. Best moods: happy, focused, relaxed.
Rationale: Driving benefits from tracks that maintain alertness without over-stimulating.
Songs with clear vocals and consistent energy work well for highway driving. For city
commutes, slightly lower energy prevents stress amplification.

PARTY / SOCIAL GATHERING
Target energy: 0.75–1.0. Target valence: 0.65–1.0.
Ideal BPM: 115–145. Danceability: HIGH.
Best genres: EDM, latin, pop. Best moods: happy, party, intense.
Rationale: High energy and positive valence create social energy. Danceability is
the primary driver — tracks with strong, consistent grooves keep crowds engaged.

DINNER PARTY
Target energy: 0.4–0.65. Target valence: 0.5–0.75.
Ideal BPM: 85–115. No strong genre preference.
Best genres: Jazz, R&B, pop, acoustic. Best moods: relaxed, chill, focused.
Rationale: Background music for conversation should fill silence without dominating.
Moderate energy prevents the music from demanding attention.

MEDITATION / YOGA
Target energy: 0.1–0.35. Target valence: 0.4–0.7.
Ideal BPM: 50–80. Acousticness: very HIGH. Instrumentalness: HIGH.
Best genres: Ambient, acoustic. Best moods: ambient, chill.
Rationale: The goal is non-interference. Music should recede into the background
and support mindfulness without triggering emotional responses or cognitive engagement.
